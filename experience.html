Experience

You can find below my experience across academia and industry listed in reverse chronological order.


Vector Institute

Machine Learning Associate at Vector Institute

As a Machine Learning Associate at the Vector Institute, I developed and deployed machine learning models to forecast product demand for ThisFish, a seafood traceability software company. The project involved working with complex datasets from seafood processors to generate accurate weekly and daily forecasts, optimizing order fulfillment and enhancing efficiency for their clients. I employed a range of advanced forecasting methods, including statistical models, ensemble learning techniques (XGBoost, LightGBM, Random Forest), and neural networks like N-BEATS. The findings and outcomes of this project were instrumental in helping seafood processors optimize procurement, production, and staffing, thereby reducing waste and improving profitability.

Queen's University

Research Assistant at the AIIM Lab, Queen's University

I was a Research Assistant at Queen's University from September 2020 to May 2023, working under the supervision of Prof. Ali Etemad and Prof. Javad Hashemi while completing my master's in Artificial Intelligence in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering. My research focused on designing clinical deep learning algorithms that are less dependent on data, labels, and medical supervision. My work has been published in top-tier journals and conferences, including IEEE Journal of Biomedical and Health Informatics and International Joint Conference on Neural Networks.

Thesis publications

IEEE World Congress on Computational Intelligence

Oral Presenter

I had the opportunity to present my research paper titled "Analysis of Augmentations for Contrastive ECG Representation Learning" at the International Joint Conference on Neural Networks (IJCNN) during the 2022 IEEE World Congress on Computational Intelligence. This paper was also published in the conference proceedings by IEEE. The presentation focused on optimizing ECG data augmentations for self-supervised contrastive learning, contributing valuable insights to the field of biomedical signal processing.

Avir

As a Machine Learning Researcher at Avir from February 2019 to June 2020, I focused on image dataset curation, leveraging web scraping and comprehensive data cleaning techniques to develop diverse and high-quality datasets. I also engineered a ResNet-based framework using PyTorch to enhance affect recognition from facial images, applying advanced computer vision techniques to achieve accurate predictions.

University of Tehran

During my Bachelor's degree at the University of Tehran, I engaged deeply with courses such as machine learning, intelligent systems, and pattern recognition. I also had the opportunity to serve as a teaching assistant for various courses, including Computer Systems and Programming, Intelligent Systems, Digital Signal Processing, Microprocessors. My hands-on experience in the Secure Communication Laboratory involved projects like speaker recognition tasks, emotion recognition using deep learning techniques, and generating face datasets with GANs. I also contributed to a project focused on predicting the rate of cerebral autoregulation of blood flow and pressure using Convolutional Recurrent Neural Networks in Python.
